Privacy Policy

App: Math Stars: Kids Learning Games
Developer: RobboTech
Effective date: June 15, 2026
Last updated: June 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Math Stars: Kids Learning Games ("the App", "we", "us", or "our") handles information when you or your child use the App. We are committed to protecting the privacy of children and complying with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), the Google Play Families Policy, and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including GDPR-K.

The App is designed for children and families. We do not require account creation, and we do not knowingly collect personal information that directly identifies a child (such as full name, email, phone number, address, or photographs).

1. Information Stored on the Device

The following information is stored only locally on your device and is never transmitted to us or any third party:

  • The display name or nickname you choose to enter (optional)
  • The avatar you select
  • Learning progress, scores, stars, and daily streak data
  • App settings and preferences (such as theme)

This data remains on the device and is deleted when you uninstall the App.

2. Information Collected Automatically by Third-Party Services

We use trusted third-party services that may collect limited information to keep the App running, improve it, and (unless removed by purchase) display age-appropriate advertising. These are:

Google AdMob (Advertising)

The App displays ads through Google AdMob. Because this is a children's app, all ad requests are tagged for child-directed treatment and limited to G-rated, non-personalized content. AdMob may collect limited device data (such as device type, general app-usage signals, and a non-personalized advertising identifier) solely to serve and measure these ads. We do not allow personalized or interest-based advertising to children. See Google's Privacy Policy.

Google Firebase (Analytics, Crashlytics, Remote Config, Cloud Messaging)

We use Firebase services from Google to understand general, aggregated app usage, diagnose crashes, deliver configuration, and send notifications. Firebase may collect device information (such as device model, operating-system version, app version, and anonymous usage events). This data is aggregated and is not used to identify individual children. See Firebase Privacy and Security.

Google Play Billing (In-App Purchases)

The App offers an optional one-time purchase to remove ads and unlock content. Payments are processed entirely by Google Play. We do not receive or store your payment card details. See Google's Privacy Policy.

3. Push and Local Notifications

The App may send notifications (for example, friendly streak reminders and learning challenges). These are scheduled locally on the device. You can disable notifications at any time in your device settings.

4. How Information Is Used

  • To save and display your child's learning progress on the device
  • To keep the App stable and fix crashes
  • To understand general, anonymous usage so we can improve the App
  • To display age-appropriate, non-personalized ads (unless removed by purchase)
  • To send optional learning reminders

5. Information We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect a child's real name, email, phone number, or address.
  • We do not collect precise location.
  • We do not collect photos, contacts, or microphone/camera data.
  • We do not enable behavioral or interest-based advertising for children.
  • We do not sell personal information.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent any information. Limited data is processed only by the third-party service providers listed above (Google AdMob, Google Firebase, Google Play Billing) for the purposes described, and only in compliance with their child-directed and Families policies.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

Locally stored data (name, progress, settings) is kept on the device until you clear the App's data or uninstall the App. To delete all locally stored information, simply uninstall the App. For data held by third-party services, please refer to their privacy policies linked above.

8. Parental Rights

Parents and guardians may, at any time:

  • Review the information available within the App (it is stored on the device)
  • Delete all data by uninstalling the App or clearing App data in device settings
  • Disable notifications through device settings
  • Remove ads through the optional in-app purchase
  • Contact us with any questions or requests using the email below

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to protect information. However, no method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Continued use of the App after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
